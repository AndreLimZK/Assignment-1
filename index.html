About Us

Learn more about our history as a band and our journey in the music industry!

1994-1997: Band Formation

Snow Patrol was originally formed in 1994 under the name Shrug by University of Dundee students Gary Lightbody, Mark McClelland, and Michael Morrison. They changed their name to Polarbear in 1996 and released the Starfighter Pilot EP in 1997. Later that year, they adopted the name Snow Patrol to avoid conflicts with another band.

1997-2005: Management Issues

Snow Patrol faced significant changes in band members and management during their early years. In 1997, drummer Jonny Quinn joined after Michael Morrison left, and they signed with Jeepster, but were dropped in 2001. In 2002, guitarist Nathan Connolly joined, and with the help of new management and producer Jacknife Lee, they released their breakthrough album Final Straw in 2003.

2005-2018: Worldwide Success

In 2005, Paul Wilson replaced bassist Mark McClelland, and keyboardist Tom Simpson became an official member. They released Eyes Open, featuring the hit single "Chasing Cars," which gained massive popularity after being featured on Grey's Anatomy and became the best-selling UK album of that year. In 2011, they released Falling Empires, with Johnny McDaid joining as a full member during the tour.

2018-2023: End of an Era

Snow Patrol release Wildness, the EP What If This Is All the Love You Ever Get?, and the compilation album Reworked in 2019 and a supporting tour. On 1 September 2023, Lightbody announced that longtime members Jonny Quinn and Paul Wilson had left the band. Snow Patrol continued as a trio consisting of Lightbody, Nathan Connolly, and Johnny McDaid.

2024-Present: New Beginnings

On 29 May 2024, Snow Patrol unveiled "The Beginning", the first single from their Fraser T. Smith-produced album, The Forest Is the Path, was released on 13 September 2024.
